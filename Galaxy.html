Research Interests

Modeling the Galaxy

Pulsar dispersion measure (DM) depends on the distance to the pulsar and the electron density distributed across that distance. DMs combined with distance measurements to pulsars are therefore powerful tools for mapping the distribution of free electrons in the Milky Way Galaxy. Using the most up-to-date sample of pulsar distances and DMs, I have constructed a new model for the electron density distribution of the Galactic thick disk to incorporate turbulent density fluctuations and clumps and voids in the ISM. My model places new constraints on the internal properties of HII regions that lie in front of some pulsars, which cause their DMs to be much larger than we might expect if the ISM were homogeneous.
Ocker, S.K, Cordes, J.M., & Chatterjee, S. (2020)
